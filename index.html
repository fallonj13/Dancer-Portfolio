About

My name is Tiffany Ramirez

Dancer|Choreographer|Artist

Tiffany Ramirez was born and raised in Whittier, Los Angeles, CA. She credits her love for dance under her Highschool teacher Alia Harlan, and Rio Hondo’s College artistic director, Alyson Cartagena.

While attending Rio Hondo, she collaborated and performed for Joel Smith, Carol McDowell, and Rachel Lopez. She has shown multiple choreographic works at the American College Dance Association and worked in Disneyland’s entertainment department as a performer for seven years. In 2014, Tiffany received an Alvin Ailey summer scholarship in Monterey Bay where she studied under Ray Radio and learned oral history from Sylvia Waters.

In 2019, Tiffany moved to Seattle and was accepted into Cornish College of Arts dance dept and graduated with a BFA in 2021. While at Cornish, she has had the pleasure to train under Michelle Miller, Alfonso Cervera, Robert Campbell, and Wade Madsen.

Who I Am

Battles to Fight Beauty to Find
The world is our Playground, I explore, see and make
MAKE SOME SHIT SEE IF IT WORKS AND GO FROM THERE.

My exploration comes from playing outside, going on random adventures and seeing what the universe shows to play with. As an improvisation creator , I enjoy taking different ideas, concepts, and overall what the little kid in me wants to do. Through these experiences and explorations this work turns to stage and or film. My latest film for my Capstone BFA started with improvisation at a subway station. I then incorporated that material into a film dealing with today’s times during this pandemic. I also like to incorporate silk veil, silk fans and hula hoops into my work. Through collaboration, I work with other artists to create mediums using photography, dance and poetry as jumping off point. I enjoy using these seem ideas in my teachings and choreographic process. Imagination, play, and exploration are my values for my creative journey.
